Professional Coffee Brewing Guide

Understanding extraction variables improves consistency, flavor clarity, and balance.

1. Core Brewing Variables

  • Grind Size: Controls extraction speed.
  • Water Temperature: Ideal range: 90–96°C.
  • Brew Ratio: Coffee to water proportion.
  • Time: Directly impacts strength and bitterness.

2. Brew Ratios Reference

  • Espresso: 1:2 (18g coffee → 36g yield)
  • Pour Over: 1:15 to 1:17
  • French Press: 1:15
  • Cold Brew: 1:5 concentrate (dilute before serving)

3. Espresso Extraction

Espresso is brewed under 9 bars of pressure with a fine grind size. Standard extraction time is 25–30 seconds.

  • Dose: 18–20g
  • Yield: 36–40g
  • Extraction: 25–30 seconds

4. Manual Brewing (Pour Over)

Pour over allows full control of bloom and flow rate.

  • Grind: Medium
  • Bloom: 30–45 seconds
  • Total brew time: 3–4 minutes
  • Water temp: 92–94°C

5. Immersion Brewing (French Press)

  • Grind: Coarse
  • Steep time: 4 minutes
  • Press gently to avoid over extraction

6. Cold Brew Extraction

Cold brew uses time instead of temperature for extraction.

  • Grind: Coarse
  • Ratio: 1:5 concentrate
  • Steep: 12–18 hours (room temperature or refrigerated)

7. Understanding Extraction Balance

Under-extracted coffee tastes sour and thin. Over-extracted coffee tastes bitter and dry. Proper extraction results in sweetness, balance, and clarity.
